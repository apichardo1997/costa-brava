Barcelona → Cadaqués · 23–26 July · for two

Costa Brava

Beaches, parties, the most photogenic villages in Catalonia, and long seafood lunches, planned as one drive up the coast.

Barcelona1 h 20 → Tossa20 min → Lloret1 h → Calella · Begur1 h 15 → Cadaqués2 h 20 → Barcelona

Rent a car. It's the one decision that shapes the whole trip: buses from Estació del Nord exist, but the prettiest coves are unreachable without wheels, and in July beach parking fills by 10 am. Leave early every day, swim before the crowds, and save the towns for golden hour when the light gets good.

Day 1 Thu 23 July

Tossa de Mar, then a big night in Lloret

Barcelona → Tossa · 1 h 20 via C-32

Morning

Platja Gran, Tossa de Mar Swim

Claim sand early under the castle walls. If it fills up, Cala Pola and Cala Giverola are ten minutes north and far quieter.

Afternoon

Vila Vella old town PhotoFood

Walk up into the 14th-century walled town. The lookout by the lighthouse frames the turquoise bay through the battlements; shoot it from late afternoon when the stone goes gold. Dinner inside the walls.

Night

Lloret de Mar Party

Twenty minutes south to the coast's nightlife capital: Tropics and Disco Colossos are the big rooms, with the whole bar strip along the beach before them. A Thursday doesn't matter here — in July the clubs run every night. Sleep in Lloret or Tossa.

Day 2 Fri 24 July

The postcard coves: Calella de Palafrugell to Begur

Lloret → Calella de Palafrugell · 1 h via C-31

Morning

Calella de Palafrugell PhotoSwim

Whitewashed fishing village with boats pulled up on the sand, arguably the most aesthetic town on the coast. Coffee on the Port Bo arches, then swim off the rocks.

Midday

Camí de Ronda to Llafranc Photo

The coastal footpath to Llafranc takes about thirty minutes, cliff views the whole way. Wear real shoes, bring water.

Lunch

Toc al Mar, Aiguablava FoodSwim

Wood-fire paella with your feet basically in the sand, on the cove with the most electric water on the Costa Brava. The hardest table of the trip: book it days ahead.

Golden hour

Begur town PhotoFood

Hilltop castle with a 360° view of everywhere you swam today, pastel Cuban-colonial houses below. Dinner on a terrace in town.

Bigger night? Platja d'Aro, thirty minutes back south, has the best clubbing after Lloret. Otherwise Begur's terraces are the classier evening. Sleep in Begur or Pals.

Day 3 Sat 25 July

Cadaqués & Cap de Creus

Begur → Cadaqués · 1 h 15

Morning

Cadaqués lanes Photo

Whitewashed alleys, slate beaches, bougainvillea against blue shutters. If you want the Dalí House at Portlligat, book the timed entry online before you go.

Midday

Cap de Creus Photo

Drive out to the lighthouse at the easternmost point of Spain: lunar rock, open sea, and the road itself is the picture.

Lunch

Long seafood lunch back in Cadaqués FoodSwim

Es Baluard on the seafront, or Compartir (ex-elBulli chefs) if you can get a table. Swim off the slate beach after.

Night

Terraces over the bay Food

Saturday here is the mellow ending: drinks on the seafront, the village lit up across the water. Sleep in Cadaqués.

If Saturday should be a club night instead, flip Days 3 and 4 — see the swap below.

Day 4 Sun 26 July

Slow morning, then home

Cadaqués → Barcelona · 2 h 20

Morning

One last swim SwimFood

Seafront breakfast, a final float in the bay, one more loop through the lanes for anything you didn't shoot.

Afternoon

The drive back

Leave by mid-afternoon or wait until after dinner: Sunday evening on the AP-7 into Barcelona is the heaviest traffic of the week in July.

Options & logistics

The swap

If Saturday should be the big night out: flip Days 3 and 4. Saturday at a Platja d'Aro beach club, sunset at Sa Tuna (the tiny cove below Begur), clubs after, sleep in Begur — then Cadaqués and Cap de Creus on Sunday before the drive home.

Book now

  • The stay. July is peak season; Begur or Calella de Palafrugell is the best single base if you'd rather not change beds twice.
  • Toc al Mar, Aiguablava — days ahead, it's the toughest table on the coast.
  • Dalí House, Portlligat — timed entry, online only.
  • The car — book pickup for early morning Day 1.

Parking rule of thumb: at any cove, arrive before 10 am or plan to walk in from the road.
